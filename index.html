    FAQs

    Identity linked email already works in closed systems today. For example, in colleges, your roll number is your email ID. PhoneMail takes the same proven idea and builds it into a single nationwide solution.

    Most email IDs are created during phone setup, often without the user even realizing it. They exist mainly to enable Play Store access or app logins, not for what email was truly meant for: a secure, digital alternative to postal mail.

    WhatsApp is a private company. Email is a system and a globally recognized legal standard. If WhatsApp were legal for official documents, the government would already be using it. The IT Act recognizes only email for this purpose. Moreover, WhatsApp is not built to organize or store documents. WhatsApp is for chatting. PhoneMail is purpose built for secure document delivery, management, and cloud storage.

    Your Gmail ID, e.g., 1234rajesh#@gmail.com, is self-created (unverified, doesn't represent your identity), so the government, banks, and insurance companies won't have it and cannot email you. But your phone number (Aadhaar-linked) is registered with them, so they can directly email the documents to it.

    Gmail is built for the corporate world, focused on professional and formal communication such as client emails or student internship applications. PhoneMail is built for the people, treating email as the digital version of postal mail, for everyday, legal, and official documents.

    Your phone number is already widely collected and registered by banks, retailers, e-commerce platforms, and other services. It's not that your phone number will be exposed by PhoneMail. And if spam were a concern, you would likely already be receiving it via WhatsApp or spam calls. Nonetheless, PhoneMail will include built-in spam control in its inbox.

    DigiLocker is narrow, storing only a few government issued documents. PhoneMail is broad. It is your single inbox for every document: tax orders, receipts, lab reports, and more. One platform. All documents.

    Think of it like postal mail. You may own multiple houses, but mail goes to the address you live at. Similarly, once government integration is in place, PhoneMail will deliver to the phone number linked to your Aadhaar.

    Yes. Log in from your laptop via our desktop app or on the web with a quick QR code scan. It works like WhatsApp in how you connect, but it is built on EDI and email standards.

    All your documents are securely stored in the cloud. Just insert your SIM into your new phone and download PhoneMail, the number is auto detected, and everything is instantly restored.

    At launch, you will enter basic details like your name. In the future, with government integration, PhoneMail will fetch your name, date of birth, and other details automatically using your mobile number. This makes setup automatic and instant, which will be especially useful for rural users.

    Initially, you pick which number to create your email ID with. Once government integration is live, the system will automatically select the number linked to your Aadhaar.
